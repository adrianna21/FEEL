abstract

This research explores the emotional habits of teenagers and young adults, specifically how they track their emotions and form mood patterns over time. We examine how personality traits and emotional patterning can help high school and early college students build sustainable habits of self-awareness and regulation. Our approach frames this problem through the lens of simplification, investigating whether a low-tech, analog device can help users log their emotions and maintain a more genuine emotional connection compared to digital alternatives.
